Panic cell

Scientific Name:

Lacrymaria Anxietas

Discovered by / at:

Discovered accidentally while experimenting on an overstimulated brain cell at NYU Shanghai, 1:20 PM by Munkhbileg Ganbold

Family:

Phagocytes. These types of cells engulf and digest other cells.

Habitat:

Unstable neural environments where stress levels are high. The Panic Cell appears when under emotional or mental pressure, such as when a student is giving an exam, doing a presentation, or when someone is talking to their crush.

Appearance:

A semi-transculent cell with unstable shape that reacts under pressure. Its membrane continuously ripples and pulses, while the nucleus shakes randomly.

Key Characteristics:

  • Changes color depending on stress level
  • Unstable, random movement.
  • Eats other calm cells.
  • Calms down when fed.
  • Pulsates faster as panic increases.

What we know so far about panic cell?
Over time the Panic Cell becomes more unstable. Its color changes from green to red as stress increases. Its movement becomes faster and more chaotic. The space around it reacts to its presence. It eats small calming cells in the neural network to reduce stress.
Guidelines for human interaction with this cell
When you press the mouse, the creature follows the cursor. You can guide it around to help it find food. It calms down after eating. When you release it, the cell moves randomly again.
